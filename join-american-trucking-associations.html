Join American Trucking Associations

Welcome

Let us help you grow!

The American Trucking Associations (ATA) is the largest and most comprehensive national trade association for the trucking industry. Since our inception in 1933, we have leveraged the strength and size of our membership, which includes a robust federation of state associations, affiliated conferences, and individuals, to successfully help our member companies grow.

Our Pillars of Service

Image

ADVOCATE

 

ADVOCATE:

As the recognized leader in transportation advocacy, ATA diligently educates and guides policymakers about the essential role that trucking plays in the economy. We have developed and nurtured relationships with key White House staff, lawmakers, and regulators within the federal government. We leverage these relationships to fight to enact laws and policies that support the future of our industry and create a healthy and prosperous environment for our member companies.

Image

ADVISE

 

ADVISE:

We provide our members with the answers, advice, and tools needed to grow and support their businesses. Our staff is comprised of subject matter experts that are available to advise and support each employee within our member companies.

Additionally, we offer complimentary access to reserve members of our leadership team for speaking engagements. These engagement opportunities allow our member companies uninterrupted time to ask deeper questions, network, and hear about ATA’s priorities firsthand

Image

CONNECT

 

CONNECT:

We know the industry landscape is always changing; therefore, it’s imperative to connect with other like-minded business owners and professionals who have vast industry knowledge. ATA hosts a variety of industry-leading conferences and meetings that brings members together to network among peers and engage in the exchange of information.

Member Types

Image

FOR-HIRE

FOR-HIRE:

A company transporting goods for compensation. As an ATA For-Hire Member, your company, from executives to operations, safety, security and human resources managers, will have access to the tools and resources needed to make informed decisions for your business.

Image

PRIVATE

PRIVATE:

A company hauling goods whose primary business activity is not transportation service. You haul your own freight, and it’s imperative that your fleet runs as cost-effectively as possible. An ATA membership provides you with the tools and resources needed to make informed decisions for your fleet.

Image

ALLIED COMPANIES

ALLIED COMPANIES:

A non-carrier company that markets goods or services to the trucking industry. As a supplier to the industry, you provide valuable products and services to the trucking industry and ATA works to connect you to a critical marketplace. ATA members benefit from the use of the ATA logo, plus a listing in ATA’s Allied Directory enables further reach on your products and services.

Image

SHIPPERS

SHIPPERS:

A company without a fleet, that is not an industry specific supplier and does not have a motor carrier division(s) within its corporate structure. As a Shipper who hires motor carriers to haul your products, it’s imperative that you stay informed of industry developments. An ATA membership provides you with the tools and resources needed to make informed decisions for your business.

Image

Moving & Storage

MOVING & STORAGE:

Licensed, independent interstate mover, van line agent, or local/intrastate mover based in the United States.  Included in membership is industry intelligence, networking, a seat at the table in formulating policies affecting companies operating heavy trucks on our nation’s roadways and highways.
